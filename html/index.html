Picture this: a stormy summer night in 1816 at Villa Diodati by Lake Geneva.Lightning cracks across the sky as Lord Byron throws down a challenge to his houseguests. Among them sits an eighteen-year-old Mary Wollstonecraft Godwin, who will soon pen a tale that reverberates through centuries. What emerged from that tempestuous evening wasn't just another gothic horror. It was Frankenstein, a revolutionary meditation on ambition, responsibility, and the terrifying consequences of playing god.

Welcome to this mini digital edition recreated by Peiying Huang and the teaching team from the Text as Data from University of Antwerp. Drawing from the treasure trove of the Shelley Godwin Archive and Bodleian Library, we've created an intimate gateway into the creation of this work of art. Here, you can trace the ink-stained evolution of a story that defined an entire genre, sparked countless adaptations, and continues to haunt our collective imagination. This digital edition brings the creation process live.

Are you ready to see how the monster is created by its authors, Mary Shelley and Percy Shelley? Whether you're a dedicated scholar dissecting every revision, a student encountering the novel creation for the first time, or simply someone fascinated by the teenage prodigy who dared to ask "What makes us human?". This digital edition using Encoding Manual is your portal into literary history. Explore, question, and perhaps discover why Shelleys' monster still speaks to us, two hundred years later, about our own deepest fears and highest hopes.The Licence is included.
